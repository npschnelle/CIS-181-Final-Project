Welcome to Lille!

Lille (or Rijsel, in Flemish) is a city in northern France, the official capital of the Nord département and the Hauts-de-France region. With a population of 1.2 million across greater Lille, there is a lot to do and see when you visit. Lille is also considered the unofficial capital of French Flanders, which means that Lille has a very distinct culture compared to the rest of France: the architecture and food is far more similar to that of Belgium and the Netherlands, though inhabitants of Lille do not speak Dutch or Flemish.

Fun Facts

  • Population (Lille Proper): 233,897
  • 9 districts
  • 4th largest metropolitan area in France
  • 3rd largest university complex in France with 100,000 students
  • Officially became part of France in 1713
  • Proximity to European Hubs, by train: 1 hour from Paris, 1.5 hours from London, 30 minutes from Brussels
